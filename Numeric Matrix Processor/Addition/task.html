Description

Matrices can be presented in code like 2D-arrays. They have many cases where they can be applied: digital image processing, graph representation, and algorithms on a graph, drawing graphics, graphics effects, applied maths, statistics, physics and much more.

The matrix general form looks like this:

                                                              \(A_{N,M} = \begin{pmatrix} A_{1,1} & ... & ... & A_{1,m} \\ A_{2,1} & ... & ... & A_{2,m} \\ ... & ... & ... & ... \\ A_{n,1} & ... & ... & A_{n,m} \end{pmatrix}\)

As you can see, it contains N rows and M columns.

Let’s start with adding matrices.

Two matrices must have an equal number of rows and columns to be added. The sum of two matrices \(A\) and \(B\) will be a matrix which has the same number of rows and columns as do \( A \) and \( B \). The sum of \( A \) and \( B \), denoted \( A + B \) or \( B + A \), is computed by adding corresponding elements of \( A \) and \( B \): \( A_{i \times j} + B_{i \times j} \). Notice that the first element in the index \( i \times j \) represents a number of rows, the second represents a number of columns. The general formula for adding two matrices are shown below:

\(P_{m,n} + Q_{m,n} = \begin{pmatrix} p_{1,1} & p_{1,2} &p_{1,3} & ... & p_{1,n} \\ p_{2,1} & p_{2,2} &p_{2,3} & ... & p_{2,n} \\ p_{3,1} & p_{3,2} &p_{3,3} & ... & p_{3,n} \\ ... & ... & ... & ... & ... \\ p_{m,1} & p_{m,2} &p_{m,3} & ... & p_{m,n} \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} q_{1,1} & q_{1,2} &q_{1,3} & ... & q_{1,n} \\ q_{2,1} & q_{2,2} &q_{2,3} & ... & q_{2,n} \\ q_{3,1} & q_{3,2} &q_{3,3} & ... & q_{3,n} \\ ... & ... & ... & ... & ... \\ q_{m,1} & q_{m,2} &q_{m,3} & ... & q_{m,n} \end{pmatrix} \)

                                     \(= \begin{pmatrix} p_{1,1} +q_{1,1}& p_{1,2} +q_{1,2}&p_{1,3} +q_{1,3}& ... & p_{1,n} +q_{1,n} \\ p_{2,1} +q_{2,1} & p_{2,2}+ q_{2,2}&p_{2,3}+q_{2,3} & ... & p_{2,n} +q_{2,n}\\ p_{3,1} +q_{3,1} & p_{3,2} +q_{3,2}&p_{3,3} +q_{3,3}& ... & p_{3,n} +q_{3,n} \\ ... & ... & ... & ... & ... \\ p_{m,1}+ q_{m,1}& p_{m,2} +q_{m,2} &p_{m,3}+q_{m,3} & ... & p_{m,n} +q_{m,n} \end{pmatrix} \)   

 

Here is the example with numbers:

                          \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 &5 & 6 \\ 6 & 6 &7 & 8 \\ 5 & 0 &0 & 1 \\ 8 & 8 &2 & 9 \\ \end{pmatrix} +\begin{pmatrix} 7 & 7 &0 & 1 \\ 9 & 9 &9 & 2 \\ 5 & 4 &3 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 &5 & 6 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2+7 & 4+7 &5+0 & 6+1 \\ 6+9 & 6+9 &7+9 & 8+2 \\ 5+5 & 0+4 &0+3 & 1+12 \\ 8+0 & 8+6 &2+5 & 9+6 \\ \end{pmatrix} \)

In this stage, you should write a program that output sum of \( A \) and \( B \) matrices.

The first line of standard input is a number of rows N and number of columns M of \( A \) matrix. Next N lines are \( A \) matrix’s elements. Next line after empty line is a number of rows N and number of columns M of \( B \) matrix. Next N lines are \( B \) matrix’s elements.

You should output the result of a sum of A and B matrices or ERROR message if it’s impossible. Input contains only integers.

Examples

Input:

4 5
1 2 3 4 5
3 2 3 2 1
8 0 9 9 1
1 3 4 5 6
4 5

1 2 3 9 8
1 0 0 0 1

Output:

2 3 7 8 10
7 6 8 9 9
9 2 12 18 9
2 3 4 5 7
